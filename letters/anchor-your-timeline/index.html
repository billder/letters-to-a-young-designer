Anchor your timeline

Where does your life fall within the greater timeline?

One core foundation of this text is becoming aware of where your life falls within the greater timeline that surrounds us. How do our individual lives overlap the general timeline of history, both past and future?

Thinking about the future is naturally difficult and weird, fraught with epic guesswork. This is probably why we usually speak in generalities. We talk about retiring at 65, or we talk about what the future may look like in 2030, but we almost never mash these concepts together to glimpse at what our future may look like.

Ask yourself, how old will you be in specific future years? Let’s use some first-grade math to stitch these two futures together. The question simply becomes, in what year will you be what age? In what year will you be 32, 44, 51, 62? When you do this, things get real.

Suppose you were born in 1997. You are a 25yo reading this sentence in 2022. You will be 40yo in 2037. You will be 53yo in 2050. Those years sound far away. They sound like science fiction. But consider this, I am older than either 40 or 53. From a 25yo’s perspective, my “2037” or “2050” have already passed. My baseline timeline is shifted earlier.

Time is weird.

A 25yo in 2022 will be 87yo in 2084. And if you really keep your health up and get lucky along the way, you will be a 103yo when the 22nd century arrives in 2100. It’s not that far-fetched. My great-grandmother reached 103 and she was born a hundred years before you, born in the 19th century and died in the 21st.

These year numbers that sound like science fiction immediately become anchored as real years in your own life or the life of someone you know. Do you have a young kid, say one born in 2018, a 4yo here in 2022? They will be 67yo and roughly at retirement age in 2085. Every strange prediction you hear about the future will potentially be a part of their everyday life. That cute little kid you know will likely see the 22nd century. Simmer on that.

In fact, there are easily cute little 5yo’s out there today who will have grandchildren that live into the 23rd century. Suppose a kid born in 2017 has a child at 45yo in 2062, a highly probably likelihood, even if not at high percentages. That child then has a child of their own at 45 in 2107.  This grandchild of today’s 5yo then lives to be 93 and witnesses the year 2200, the beginning of the 23rd century.

So right now, do the math for the year you were born in and then say out loud “I will be [your age] in the year 2043.” Interesting, eh? Doesn’t it make 2043 feel just a bit more real?

You now have a personal anchor to the future.
