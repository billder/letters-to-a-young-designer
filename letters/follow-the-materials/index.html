Follow the materials

New design challenges are created when new technologies are introduced. They become new design materials to work with, whether designers like it or not. Every decade introduces new technologies that can be incorporated into solutions. Each of these technologies also create fresh societal problems as the relationship between people and technology evolves. The coming decades will be no different. We’ll see new materials and new problems.
But here’s the interesting bit. When new technologies are introduced into everyday life, they require some form of adjustment from the people who use them. They alter our engagement with the world and each other somehow.
For the simplest example, compare the world before and after cell phones became affordable enough for a critical mass to own them.

New types of problems require new combinations of skill sets to tackle them. New roles. Thus the painful evolution of job titles within the design world. Interactive designer, webmaster, information architect, user experience, etc.
